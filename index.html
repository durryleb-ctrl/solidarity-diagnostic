The Solidarity
Diagnostic

Solidarity is not static. It takes shape within relational dynamics — and the same person can be someone's deepest ally on one issue and purely surface-level on another.

This tool is not a verdict. It is a map. Score yourself honestly on two different relationships and see what the comparison reveals about where you are — and where you can grow.

How to use this tool

1
Choose who you are in solidarity with and around what issue — select as many as apply
2
Score yourself honestly on the three dimensions — no right answers, only true ones
3
Do it twice and read what the comparison reveals about where you are and where you can grow

The three dimensions — what you are scoring

Time How long have you been in relationship with this group or cause — not just aware of it, but genuinely in relationship? Solidarity built before a crisis or ask is thicker than solidarity formed in response to one.
Quality of Connection How deeply do you know both the issues AND the people living that reality — their histories, their lives, their full humanity? Real friendships, not just cause alignment. The goal is seeing the whole person, not only their struggle.
Shared Risk Are both parties genuinely putting something on the line — time, reputation, resources, comfort, or power? Deep quality of connection makes shared risk more likely and more natural. Shallow connection tends to keep risk low even when time is long.

These three dimensions interact. You can have a long relationship with shallow connection and risk will stay low. But when you deeply know people's lives and they know yours, the risk becomes naturally shared — because you can no longer unsee each other's humanity.

What the comparison reveals

You are not one type of solidarity practitioner. You are many.

How to read this: The bars show your three dimension scores side by side for each relationship. Fuller and darker bars mean thicker solidarity. Look for gaps — between relationships, and between dimensions within the same relationship. The gaps are not failures. They are your growing edge. And remember: the conditions you are working within matter. Organizational culture, power dynamics, and available resources all shape what is possible. Name those conditions alongside your scores.
